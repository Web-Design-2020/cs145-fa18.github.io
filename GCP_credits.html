Dear Students,

Google has provided all students in this class with $50 of credit for BigQuery. This should be more than ample to finish the course, possibly even with credit remaining.

Credit policies & information are as follows:

  • $50 of credit is enough to query 10 Terabytes of data ($5/TB). This is a very large amount of data for the purposes of this class. You would need to run 1000 queries on 10GB in order to exhaust this, for example.
  • Google provides all users of bigquery an additional 1TB free / month. You may find that you don’t even use 1TB over the course of the first two projects.
  • You are responsible for your credit. If you are in danger of running out (eg, you are running $2 queries) please contact the TAs. We are able to help students before they use up their credits, but there’s not much we can do after you’ve used them up.
  • Google charges by # of rows * # of columns * size of column for each query. The easiest and best way to keep the amount of data you handle down is to use only the columns you need for your query. It can be a little verbose at times, but if you stick to the practice of writing SELECT column1, column2…, you will save lots of credits over the course of the quarter.
  • Corollary: AVOID USING SELECT *. Google will charge the query as scanning the whole table, even if it doesn’t.
